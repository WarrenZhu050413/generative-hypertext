Direct Manipulation Evolutionary Chain

1963 Ivan Sutherland - Sketchpad demonstrates direct manipulation of graphical objects (first instance)
1970 Alan Kay - Desktop metaphor and Smalltalk at Xerox PARC (implementation)
1983 Ben Shneiderman - Formalizes and names "direct manipulation" concept (theorization)

Key Insight: Shneiderman did not invent direct manipulation - he formalized and named an existing practice, explicitly acknowledging Sutherland and Xerox PARC as precedents.

Unifying Concept: Human Augmentation Through Computing
